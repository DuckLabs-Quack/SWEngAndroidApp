First steps

17/11/2017 -

Our first ever meeting occored on the 17th of November 2017, where we introduced ourselves and got to know one another. During this meeting we outlined our individual strengths and weaknesses as well as how we felt they could be best deployed in the form of group roles. We also made it a priority to decide on the group's objective in terms of what kind of app we would develop.

Generating product ideas

17/11/2017 - 18/01/2018

Prior to the first meeting, we agreed on bringing any project ideas to be presented and discussed by the team. The ideas presented were:

  • App to teach someone to play a musical instrument (one that canâ€™t play chords)
  • App for climbing to find nearby spots, and possibly expand to share climbing achievements on social media and find other climbing enthusiasts
  • App to teach children tips and tricks for a device like a raspberry pi
  • App to teach someone sign language.
We discussed these ideas at length, outlining advantages and disadvantages in each. After realising there was no clear victor idea, we decided to give in-depth mini-presentations of our favourite ideas at our following meeting (18/01/2018), some of which were new, and have an election to decide.

The final ideas presented were:

  • Lab helper app that gives the user useful tools when working with electronics
  • App-based cook book
  • App-based travel guide.
After a round of voting, there was a clear majority for the cook book idea.

Now that we had an idea of what we wanted to do, we were in a position to decide on team roles, and who would occupy them.

Documentation Process

After assigning group roles, we needed to start planning ahead for the project ahead. A slack group was made for all group communications, and a shared online calendar to keep track of deadlines and events.

QA Manual

01/02/2018 onwards

A thoroughly thought out QA Manual is imperative to the group's success as without it roles and tasks are not properly defined and therefore group members will be unable to focus on what is required from them. The First Iteration of the QA Manual was edited and compiled by the QA & Documentation manager with all individual group members providing the content for their respective sections; not that the Software Manager and QA/Doc Manager created the role definition for Software Engineer.

As the project progressed it became clear that changes were required; the initial version and the current version of the QA Manual are available here as well as some notes documenting the changes between the two.

QA Self-Assessment Sheets 1 & 2

Twice during the project, QA self-assessment sheets were filled in by all group members and then reviewed by the QA Manager to ensure that work was being conducted effectively with no internal issues. Any points made on these sheets were brought up and discussed in the meetings.

The filled in Self-Assessment sheets, template and review document are available on the Documentation page of this website here

Project plan & projection

05/02/2018 - 21/02/2018

One of the Team Leader's first major tasks was to outline a project plan, outlining the timing and duration of all tasks to be undertaken. These figures would be used in estimating costs, as well as providing a foundational plan to follow to ensure the success of the project.

After an initial prediction and cost analysis was completed, team members raised the concern that the hours were far too optimistic, outlining the difference between our predictions and the predictions of other teams. This led to a project plan overhaul, re-estimating the task durations with worst-case scenarios in mind. For comments, see the project overview document of the Team Leader (Marco Bolt).

Defining product specification

18/01/2018 - 02/02/2018

From meeting 2 (18/01/2018) we already had a good idea of what we wanted the product to do. A set of basic functionalities were outlined as follows (Taken directly from the meeting minutes):

  • The app is a cooking app with recipes available for the user to have access to via a server
  • A shopping list will be available for each recipe, and available to be downloaded for when there isnâ€™t an internet connection
  • One of the main USPâ€™s of the product is that it displays the recipe instructions/steps in a slideshow format, and that the user can switch between steps in a manner (to be finalised) that would require minimal usage of their hands, which would be useful for many recipes

Implementation

12/02/2018 - present

Once our [FINANCIAL REQUEST THING] had been approved and the designs and specification were outlined, we were ready to begin development of the app. As outlined in the QA Manual, we deployed an Agile programming methodology, following a series of programmer stories in realising our designs and specifications.

Version 1: Prototype

15/01/2018 - 16/03/2018

The development of the prototype could be split into several sections:

  • Server
  • Functionality
  • GUI

Server

The server was written in python utilising the "Flask" microframework. This was a system that would handle basic http requests, and pass information to any connected instances of the app.

Functionality

The functionality development consisted largely of data handling and management. For the prototype, this included:

  • Data handling
  • XML Parsing
  • Filtering

GUI

GUI development saw the generation of the page template files, and code that interfaced with it. The tasks involved in GUI implementation for the prototype included:

  • Layout descriptions (XML)
  • Activity code (making each page displayable in the app)
  • Interfacing with objects on screen
